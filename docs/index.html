Mirror Gold Wiki

Pokémon Mirror Gold is a doubles-focused QoL and difficulty hack of HeartGold, built using hg-engine. Most Pokémon gens 1-9 are available throughout the game both in the wild and featured in hand-crafted trainer battles. Weaker Pokémon have received buffs to bring power levels in line and balance things across the board. Many QoL features like not needing HMs, Portable PC, Infinite Rare Candy are present with more being added over time.

TL;DR Features

  • All trainer battles are Double Battles!
  • Fully rebalanced gen 1-9 roster
  • Optional Permadeath/Hardcore mode
  • Mega Evolution
  • 150+ TMs and 50+ TRs available in-game
  • Portable PC with R button
  • Massive HM QoL improvements

Trainer Changes

  • Check out the Mirror Gold Trainerdex
  • Fully hand-crafted trainer teams designed to introduce challenge and variety
  • Trainer levels are scaled based on your party
  • Slightly enhanced trainer AI (more to come)

Pokémon Changes

  • Check out the Mirror Gold Pokédex
  • Stats, types, abilities, and role changes for all Pokémon
  • Wiki contains location information for each Pokémon. All should be available before the Elite 4
  • More Eviolite Pokémon are now viable paired with QoL of Eviolite now preventing evolution when held
  • Every non-legendary Pokémon has been rebalanced with the goal of making all Pokémon viable
  • Greater Type Diversity
  • Fairy Typing

Item Changes

  • Held items (except berries) are returned to you at the end of the battle
  • Cannot use items in trainer battles (held items are fine)
  • Pokémon Marts contain many new items so be sure to check them out
  • Nature-changing mints are available throughout the game in Poké Marts
  • Eviolite prevents regular evolution

System Changes

  • Optional and toggleable Permadeath mode
  • Huge field move QoL changes (no longer need to know the HMs, Fly available right away on Flying mons)
  • Free healing items on visiting a Pokécenter to reduce time spent there
  • "Lucky" IVs means 30 IVs is the most common value
  • EV training has been eliminated all Pokémon grant 2 EVs in all stats
  • EV/IV viewer in the Pokémon stats panel in-game by pressing L or R
  • Evasion increases are no longer present in the game (though accuracy drops still exist)
  • Move rebalance - typically reducing RNG elements and equalizing power a bit
  • Many new moves - thanks to hg-engine
  • More open map
